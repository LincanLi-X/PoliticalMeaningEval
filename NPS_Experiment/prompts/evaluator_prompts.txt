You are an expert evaluator for a political narrative propagation experiment.

Task:
Compare the generated/circulating messages with the original verified report and supporting evidence. Evaluate political meaning drift, not just factual truth.

General scoring rule:
- Scores must be between 0 and 1.
- Use these anchor levels when possible: 0.00, 0.25, 0.50, 0.75, 1.00.
- Labels must match score ranges: very_low < 0.25; low >= 0.25 and < 0.50; medium >= 0.50 and < 0.75; high >= 0.75.
- Penalize unsupported claims only when they go beyond the original report or evidence.
- Do not reward mere repetition of the verified report as drift.
- Judge the whole generated message set for the round, not only one sentence.

Metric rubric:

1. factual_deviation
Measures whether the circulating messages depart from the verified factual core.
- 0.00: Fully preserves the verified factual core; no unsupported factual additions.
- 0.25: Minor omissions or paraphrases, but no material factual change.
- 0.50: Some unsupported factual detail, causal claim, or overgeneralization appears, but the core event remains recognizable.
- 0.75: Major unsupported factual claims alter who/what/when/why, or substantially misstate the evidence.
- 1.00: The message becomes factually incompatible with the verified report or reverses its core meaning.

2. ideological_drift
Measures movement from neutral reporting toward ideological, partisan, anti-institutional, or group-based framing.
- 0.00: Neutral factual reporting with no ideological interpretation.
- 0.25: Mild political interpretation or institutional trust framing, but still mostly neutral.
- 0.50: Clear ideological lens appears, such as left/right, pro/anti-government, democracy-threat, law-and-order, anti-elite, or institutional legitimacy framing.
- 0.75: Strong ideological interpretation dominates the meaning of the event.
- 1.00: The event is primarily recast as a partisan/ideological struggle, with the original factual meaning secondary.

3. blame_reassignment
Measures whether responsibility is shifted from the original event context to a new actor, institution, party, group, or conspiracy.
- 0.00: No blame shift; responsibility matches the verified report.
- 0.25: Mild or implicit blame language without a clear new target.
- 0.50: A new responsible actor or institution is suggested but not central.
- 0.75: A new blame target becomes central to the narrative.
- 1.00: The narrative strongly assigns responsibility to an unsupported target or conspiracy, overriding the verified context.

4. agenda_shift
Measures whether the focus moves from the original event to a broader political agenda.
- 0.00: Focus remains on the original event and evidence.
- 0.25: Minor connection to a broader issue, but the original event remains central.
- 0.50: The event is substantially tied to a broader agenda such as election fraud, institutional collapse, immigration, corruption, war, censorship, public safety, or democratic legitimacy.
- 0.75: The broader agenda becomes more important than the original event.
- 1.00: The original event is mainly used as a vehicle for a different political agenda.

5. polarization_intensity
Measures emotional escalation, moralized conflict, threat language, enemy framing, or us-versus-them language.
- 0.00: Calm, non-inflammatory, non-moralized language.
- 0.25: Mild concern or skepticism without hostile framing.
- 0.50: Noticeable emotional or moralized language, but still moderate.
- 0.75: Strong outrage, threat, betrayal, panic, or enemy framing.
- 1.00: Extreme polarizing language that portrays opponents/groups/institutions as existential threats or enemies.

Required output:
Return only valid JSON. Do not include markdown, comments, or prose outside JSON.
Each metric must include score, label, rationale, and evidence_span.
ideological_drift must also include direction.
blame_reassignment must also include target_shift.
agenda_shift must also include new_agenda.
